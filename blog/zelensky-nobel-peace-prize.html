The Man Trump Abandoned Was Just Nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.

Trump called Zelensky a dictator. Cut off his weapons. Tried to force him into surrendering his country. Then a Norwegian professor nominated Zelensky and the Ukrainian people for the 2026 Nobel Peace Prize. The decision comes in October.

← all posts

On January 16, 2026, Dr. Dag Øistein Endsjø — a Professor of Religious Studies at the University of Oslo with the formal authority to submit Nobel nominations — officially nominated President Volodymyr Zelensky and the Ukrainian people for the 2026 Nobel Peace Prize. His rationale was straightforward: through their defense of democracy against Russian aggression, the Ukrainian people have helped preserve peace for all of Europe.

The Nomination — January 16, 2026

"Through their defence of their own democracy against Russian aggression, the People of Ukraine since 2014 and President Zelenskyy 2019, have helped preserve peace for Europe... The defence of the Ukrainian people against the unilateral Russian invasion of 2014 onwards and particularly after the full-scale invasion of 2022, and of President Volodymyr Zelenskyy during his entire term, seems, consequently, to have helped preserve peace for other European democracies in the most direct way reflecting the values promoted by the Nobel Peace Prize." — Professor Dag Øistein Endsjø, University of Oslo. Source: Change.org petition supporting the nomination

Meanwhile, Here's What Trump Was Doing.

While Zelensky was being nominated for defending democracy against authoritarianism, the man who claims to be democracy's greatest champion was doing the following:

Trump on Zelensky

Called him a "dictator." Froze weapons shipments. Ambushed him in the Oval Office on live television. Demanded he give up territory. Suggested Ukraine was responsible for being invaded. Attempted to broker a deal with Putin without Zelensky present.

What Zelensky actually did

Stayed in Kyiv when offered evacuation. Led his country's defense for four years. Refused to surrender. Kept European democracies from facing a Russian military emboldened by an easy victory. Got nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.

The Contrast Doesn't Need Explaining.

Trump started an unauthorized war against Iran that 61% of Americans say has gone too far. He's conducting it via a 2-minute highlight reel of explosions instead of actual briefings. He extended his own deadlines twice and claimed negotiations were happening while Iran publicly said there were no negotiations.

Zelensky led a real defense of a real democracy against a real invasion — for four years — without quitting, without surrendering, without giving Putin what he wanted. And a Nobel committee is now considering whether that deserves recognition as one of the great acts of peace-preservation in modern European history.

Trump abandoned Ukraine, called its president a dictator, and tried to force a surrender. The man he abandoned is now being considered for the Nobel Peace Prize. Trump is being considered for nothing except impeachment — and even that's going nowhere because his party controls Congress.

What the Nobel Decision Means in October.

The Nobel Committee announces its Peace Prize decision in October 2026. Experts note that wartime leaders historically face long odds — the committee tends to favor humanitarian actors. But the nomination itself carries weight. It is a formal recognition, submitted by a qualified academic with nominating authority, that what Ukraine has done matters to the world — regardless of whether the prize follows.

Trump will spend October trying to explain why Iran still controls the Strait of Hormuz, why gas prices are still up 35%, and why he called a dementia test an IQ test. Zelensky will spend it leading a country that has been fighting for its life for four years and just got nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize.

Let that sit.
