First Term Record — 2020 COVID lies 400,000 dead fact-checked

Trump Told Bob Woodward COVID Was Deadly and Airborne in February 2020. He Told the American Public the Opposite for Months.

On February 7, 2020 — on tape — Trump told journalist Bob Woodward that COVID was "deadly stuff," that it was "more deadly than your strenuous flu," and that it was transmitted through the air. For the next several months, he told the American public it would disappear in April, it was no worse than the flu, it was a Democratic hoax, and masks didn't work. He later explained his public messaging by saying he didn't want to cause "panic." 400,000 Americans were dead by the time he left office. All of this is documented. Much of it is on tape, in his own voice.

📁 First Term Record — documented history

Bob Woodward is one of the most experienced journalists in American history. When he interviews presidents, he records the conversations. He did so during 18 interviews with Donald Trump between December 2019 and July 2020, for a book called "Rage." The recordings exist. They are not disputed. On February 7, 2020 — when the United States had fewer than a dozen confirmed COVID cases and Trump was publicly saying the virus was "very much under control" — Trump told Woodward something very different in private.

"It's also more deadly than — you know, your strenuous flus. This is more deadly. This is five per — this is five percent versus one percent and less than one percent. So this is deadly stuff."

— Donald Trump, in a private recorded conversation with Bob Woodward, February 7, 2020

Trump also told Woodward the virus was airborne — "It goes through air, Bob" — weeks before public health officials were publicly emphasizing aerosol transmission. On March 19, 2020, as the US death toll climbed and states began shutting down, Trump told Woodward: "I wanted to always play it down. I still like playing it down, because I don't want to create a panic." He said this while the virus was actively killing Americans.

The Additional Failures Beyond the Lies.

The Woodward recordings document the knowing deception. But the policy failures compounded the damage. Trump promoted hydroxychloroquine — an anti-malarial drug — as a COVID treatment despite no clinical evidence and warnings from his own FDA. He mused publicly about whether injecting bleach or shining ultraviolet light inside the body might work as treatments — at an official White House briefing. He undermined his own public health officials, contradicting Dr. Fauci and Dr. Birx in real time at press briefings. He refused to model mask-wearing and actively discouraged it, telling Americans that masks were optional while knowing the virus was airborne. His administration failed to invoke the Defense Production Act early enough to address critical shortages of PPE, ventilators, and testing supplies. The United States, with the most advanced public health infrastructure in the world, had some of the worst per-capita death rates in the developed world during 2020.

What He Said When Confronted.

When the Woodward recordings were released in September 2020, Trump defended his deception directly. He said he was trying to be a "cheerleader for the country" and didn't want to cause "panic." He said keeping the public calm was the right call. He did not dispute that he had told Woodward something different from what he was telling the public. He simply argued the deception was justified. More than 400,000 Americans died of COVID before he left office on January 20, 2021. He held a rally in Washington DC on January 6 — two weeks before the end of his term — and no mention was made of the dead.
