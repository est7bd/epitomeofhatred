June 6, 2018 veterans VA lies fact-checked

Trump Claimed He Passed Veterans Choice. Obama Signed It in 2014.

Donald Trump told veterans, at rallies, in interviews, and in official remarks, that he had passed the Veterans Choice Program — allowing veterans to see private doctors rather than waiting for VA appointments. He said it more than 150 times over his first term. The Veterans Access, Choice and Accountability Act was signed into law by President Barack Obama on August 10, 2014. Trump did not take office until January 20, 2017. He signed an expansion of the program in 2018. Taking credit for creating it is simply false.

This is one of Trump's most persistent and easily verified lies — which is part of what makes it remarkable. The Veterans Choice Program has a signing date. That date is August 10, 2014. Barack Obama is in the photographs. The law was passed in response to a VA wait-time scandal that unfolded during Obama's presidency, and it passed with broad bipartisan support. None of this is obscure or contested. It is basic documented history.

What He Actually Said.

Trump's typical formulation went something like this: "I got Veterans Choice passed. For 40 years, people were trying to get Veterans Choice. I got it done." Variations of this claim appeared in campaign rallies, White House events, interviews with Fox News, and official presidential addresses throughout his first term. The Washington Post tracked the claim more than 150 times. Each time, fact-checkers noted it was false. Trump continued saying it.

When confronted with the facts, Trump and his allies typically pointed to the VA MISSION Act, which Trump signed in June 2018 and which significantly expanded and reformed the Veterans Choice Program. The MISSION Act was a genuine and meaningful piece of legislation that broadened eligibility and improved the program. Trump deserves credit for signing it. But the MISSION Act was an expansion of a program that already existed — a program signed by Obama. Trump did not create Veterans Choice. He improved it. These are different things, and he knew it.

Why It Matters to Veterans.

Veterans are not props. They are people who served the country and who have specific, concrete interests in accurate information about their healthcare options and who actually advocates for them. When Trump repeatedly told veterans that he had created the program that allows them to see private doctors — when Obama had actually done that — he was not just lying about policy history. He was taking credit away from the president who actually signed the law to benefit politically from the credit himself.

The broader context matters too: Trump's record on veterans is genuinely mixed. He signed the MISSION Act, which was good for veterans. He also proposed significant cuts to the VA budget in his first term. He presided over reports of continued access problems at VA facilities. He called fallen soldiers "suckers" and "losers" according to multiple former officials — a claim he denies. He made veterans the centerpiece of his political brand while taking credit for things he did not do and avoiding accountability for things he did.

Verification note

The Veterans Access, Choice and Accountability Act signing date (August 10, 2014) is documented on congress.gov and whitehouse.gov archives. Trump's 150+ repetitions of the false claim were tracked by the Washington Post Fact Checker. The VA MISSION Act of 2018 is also documented on congress.gov. Both claims — that he created Veterans Choice, and that he signed an expansion of it — are verifiable from public government records.
