March 25, 2026 DHS shutdown ICE fact-checked

Republicans Held TSA Hostage for ICE. Congress Only Cared When Their Airport Fast Lane Got Taken Away.

Democrats offered five separate bills to fund TSA, FEMA, the Coast Guard, and CISA while ICE negotiations continued. Republicans blocked every single one. For 40 days, you stood in three-hour lines. Then Delta suspended Congress's VIP airport lane. Suddenly everyone got very interested in finding a deal.

Here is the DHS shutdown in one paragraph: ICE agents are fully funded — they have access to $75 billion in separate funds Congress handed them in the "One Big Beautiful" reconciliation bill. TSA agents are not getting paid. Democrats offered to fund TSA, FEMA, the Coast Guard, CISA, and every other DHS agency except ICE's deportation operations while negotiations continued. Republicans said no. Five times. Then Delta took away Congress's special airport security bypass. Now there's suddenly momentum on a deal.

The Five Bills Republicans Blocked. In 24 Hours.

Democratic Bills Republicans Blocked — All in 24 Hours
  • Sen. Murray's bill to fund all of DHS except ICE, CBP, and the DHS Secretary's office while negotiations continue. Blocked by Sen. Katie Britt (R-AL).
  • Sen. Rosen's bill to pay TSA workers while broader negotiations continue. Blocked.
  • A bill to fund FEMA — the agency that responds to disasters. Blocked.
  • A bill to fund CISA — the agency that defends against cyberattacks. Blocked.
  • A bill to fund the US Coast Guard. Blocked.

Senator Patty Murray's response to Sen. Britt calling the TSA funding bill "defunding police": "All the bill I just offered does is fund the rest of DHS while talks continue on ICE and Border Patrol. And the simple fact is Republicans have already funded these agencies when they gave them more money — than most militaries, by the way — in their Big Ugly Bill."

"Democrats are not going to write a blank check for rogue agencies that are trampling on the rights of Americans. Nor are we going to accept the premise that the only way to fund TSA or disaster relief is by giving money to ICE. That's absurd." — Sen. Patty Murray (D-WA)

Why the Shutdown Started in the First Place.

This didn't start over a budget disagreement. The DHS shutdown started in February when Democrats voted against funding DHS in response to Operation Metro Surge — the Trump administration's immigration enforcement crackdown in Minnesota, during which federal agents shot and killed two people: Alex Pretti and Renee Good. The killing of Pretti was the 9th ICE shooting since September 2025.

Democrats' demands in exchange for funding ICE: require judicial warrants before entering homes, ban face coverings for agents, require body cameras, reduce detention bed capacity. Basic accountability measures. Republicans called this "defunding police."

How Congress Discovered Urgency.

For five weeks, airports experienced the longest TSA wait times in history. 450+ agents quit. Flights were missed. People suffered. Congress was aware of this. They held hearings. They gave speeches. And then Delta Airlines suspended its special security bypass service that allowed members of Congress to skip security lines at the airport.

The Quote That Says Everything

"Congress seemed to get really interested once Delta suspended its special service that allowed members of the House and Senate to skip security lines. Funny how that works." — The American Prospect, March 24, 2026. Source: The American Prospect

The "Deal" on the Table — And Why It's Still Not Enough.

The emerging Republican framework funds most of DHS — including TSA — but withholds only ICE's Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO) budget. The plan is to then pass ICE funding and the SAVE America Act together through budget reconciliation, bypassing Democrats entirely with a simple majority vote.

Democrats' problem with this: it includes none of the accountability reforms they've been demanding. No warrant requirements. No mask ban. No meaningful oversight. Just the money, with none of the guardrails. As Sen. Chris Murphy put it: "We, as a caucus, have said we're not funding a lawless immigration enforcement operation, and that remains the caucus position."

The vote to advance the bill failed Thursday. Congress then left for two weeks of Easter recess. TSA agents still aren't getting paid.
