December 6, 2022 Trump Organization tax fraud corruption fact-checked

The Trump Organization Was Convicted of 17 Felonies. It Was the First Criminal Conviction of a US President's Company.

On December 6, 2022, a Manhattan jury convicted the Trump Organization on all 17 counts of tax fraud, falsifying business records, and other charges. The scheme involved the Trump Organization paying its top executives off the books — providing perks like apartments, cars, and private school tuition for their children that were never reported as income — for approximately 15 years. CFO Allen Weisselberg pleaded guilty and testified for the prosecution. The organization was fined $1.6 million — the maximum the law allowed. It was the first time a business controlled by a sitting or former US president had been convicted of felonies.

The Manhattan District Attorney's investigation into the Trump Organization began in 2018 and culminated in a June 2021 indictment of the company and its longtime CFO Allen Weisselberg. The scheme was elegant in its corruption: instead of paying top executives taxable salaries, the Trump Organization paid many of their personal expenses directly — rent on luxury apartments, car leases, private school tuition for family members, furniture, and cash — and recorded these as business expenses rather than employee compensation. This let the employees pay lower taxes (they weren't declaring the income) while the company took deductions it wasn't entitled to. It ran from 2005 to 2021.

Weisselberg pleaded guilty in August 2022 and testified for the prosecution in exchange for a reduced sentence. He was sentenced to five months in jail. The Trump Organization argued that Weisselberg had acted without the knowledge or approval of Trump himself — a defense the jury rejected in convicting the corporate entities. The company was fined the maximum $1.6 million, which was widely noted as laughably small relative to the scale of a company with properties worth billions.

Trump personally was not charged in this case — District Attorney Alvin Bragg subsequently brought separate criminal charges against Trump himself relating to falsified business records connected to hush money payments to Stormy Daniels. In May 2024, Trump was convicted on all 34 counts of falsifying business records — becoming the first former US president convicted of a crime. He was sentenced to an unconditional discharge in January 2025 before returning to the White House.
