April 23, 2020 COVID-19 public health first term fact-checked

Trump Suggested Injecting Disinfectant. Poison Control Calls Spiked.

At a White House coronavirus briefing on April 23, 2020, Trump mused aloud about whether injecting disinfectant into the body could kill COVID-19, and whether doctors could investigate bringing UV light "inside the body." This happened on live television, in front of the country's top public health officials, during a pandemic that was killing thousands of Americans per day. Poison control centers reported a spike in disinfectant-related calls the following days.

The quote is on video and is not ambiguous. Trump said, in the context of discussing disinfectants killing the virus on surfaces: "And then I see the disinfectant where it knocks it out in a minute. One minute. And is there a way we can do something like that, by injection inside or almost a cleaning?" He also said: "Supposing we hit the body with a tremendous, whether it's ultraviolet or just very powerful light... and then I said, supposing you brought the light inside the body, which you can do either through the skin or in some other way."

The Immediate Response.

Dr. Deborah Birx, standing at the briefing, visibly looked down. The manufacturer of Lysol — Reckitt — issued a public statement the following day: "As a global leader in health and hygiene products, we must be clear that under no circumstance should our disinfectant products be administered into the human body (through injection, ingestion or any other route)." The New York City health department reported a spike in calls about disinfectant exposure the day after Trump's remarks. Maryland's emergency management office received hundreds of calls asking whether disinfectant could be used to treat COVID-19.

Trump's Walk-Back.

Trump initially said he was being "sarcastic" and asked a question sarcastically to reporters. The video of the briefing shows he was not speaking to reporters — he was speaking to Dr. Birx and appeared to be thinking aloud. He then said he was asking "a question sarcastically to reporters." He subsequently cancelled the daily briefings entirely, citing media coverage he found unfair.

Verification note

The quote is verbatim from the April 23, 2020 White House briefing transcript, which is publicly available. The Reckitt/Lysol statement was issued April 24, 2020 and is on record. Poison control spike data was reported by multiple state health departments and covered by AP, Washington Post, and NBC News.

This came less than two months after Trump had told Bob Woodward — in a recorded interview — that he knew COVID-19 was deadly and airborne and had deliberately downplayed it to avoid "panic." The disinfectant incident and the Woodward tapes together document an administration that was simultaneously hiding the truth from the public and improvising dangerous misinformation in real time.
