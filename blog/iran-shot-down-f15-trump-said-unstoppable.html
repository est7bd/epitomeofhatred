What Trump Said. What Then Happened.

Trump's April 1 primetime address was the administration's most definitive statement yet about US air superiority over Iran. He did not say Iran's air defenses were degraded or reduced. He said they were gone. "They have no anti-aircraft equipment." "Their radar is 100% annihilated." "We are unstoppable as a military force." Forty-eight hours later, Iran used its air defenses — the ones Trump said didn't exist — to shoot down an F-15E Strike Eagle, one of the most capable multirole fighter jets in the US Air Force arsenal, from the 494th Fighter Squadron based at RAF Lakenheath in the UK. Then they hit an A-10 sent to rescue the crew. Then they hit two Blackhawks.

The Sequence of Events on April 3.

An F-15E Strike Eagle with two crew members was shot down by Iranian fire over western Iran — the first confirmed loss of a US jet to enemy fire in this war. Both crew members ejected. US special forces located and rescued one of them alive, on Iranian territory, during an active combat search and rescue operation. The other crew member remains missing. Iran's regional governor issued a public call for locals to search for the surviving crew and offered a reward equivalent to $60,000. Iranian state media told civilians to "shoot them if you see them" and published video purportedly showing locals firing at US helicopters with firearms. During the rescue operation, a US A-10 Thunderbolt II — a heavily armored close air support aircraft — was struck by Iranian fire. The pilot made it to Kuwaiti airspace before ejecting; the aircraft crashed in Kuwait. Separately, Iranian forces hit two US Blackhawk helicopters involved in the search and rescue effort.

"Their radar is 100% annihilated. We are unstoppable." Two days later, Iran shot down an F-15, an A-10, and hit two Blackhawks. The commander-in-chief didn't know what he was talking about — and US troops paid for it.

Rep. Moulton Said It Directly.

Democratic Rep. Seth Moulton, a Marine combat veteran who serves on the House Armed Services Committee, went on CNN after the F-15 was shot down and said plainly: "The commander-in-chief doesn't know what he's talking about." He said Trump's false claims about Iran having zero air defense capability put US troops "at grave risk." Earlier, Moulton had said of the administration's approach to the Iran war: "They don't know how to get out of this mess. They do not know how to end this war." He said that in the context of a war now entering its sixth week with no exit strategy, no congressional authorization, 13 Americans killed, 365 wounded, a crew member missing inside Iran, and oil markets in chaos.

Iran’s Air Defenses Were Not “100% Annihilated.”

This is not a minor misstatement. If the president tells the nation that Iran has no air defenses and then mission planners send F-15s into Iranian airspace based on that assessment, and Iran then shoots them down, the false claim has direct operational consequences. Rep. Moulton said exactly that. NBC News reported that the downing of the F-15 "put in stark relief a growing challenge facing the president: despite a daily bombing campaign and his triumphant wartime narrative, Iran retains enough military capabilities to inflict considerable damage to US service members and America's allies and assets in the Middle East." Trump's public statements about Iranian air capabilities were not just wrong — they were dangerously, operationally wrong at a moment when American lives depended on an accurate picture of the threat.

His Response: “KEEP THE OIL, ANYONE?”

After an F-15 was shot down with one crew member still missing inside enemy territory, Trump's public response on Truth Social was a series of posts about taking Iran's oil and opening the Strait of Hormuz for profit. "KEEP THE OIL, ANYONE?" he wrote. He told NBC News that the aircraft being shot down would not affect possible negotiations with Iran. He did not address his false claim about Iranian air defenses being annihilated. He did not acknowledge the gap between what he told the nation and what Iran just demonstrated. He posted about oil.
