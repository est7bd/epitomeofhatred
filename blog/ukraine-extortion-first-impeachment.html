First Term Record — July 25, 2019 Ukraine impeachment extortion fact-checked

"I Would Like You to Do Us a Favor, Though." Trump Withheld $391 Million in Military Aid to Extort Ukraine. He Was Impeached.

On July 25, 2019, Donald Trump called Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky. After Zelensky mentioned wanting to buy more Javelin anti-tank missiles, Trump said: "I would like you to do us a favor, though." He wanted Ukraine to announce investigations into Joe Biden — his most likely 2020 opponent — and a debunked conspiracy theory about the 2016 election. The $391 million in congressionally approved military aid that Ukraine needed to fight Russian-backed separatists was being withheld as leverage. The GAO later found the withholding was illegal. Trump's own ambassador testified it was a quid pro quo. The House impeached Trump on two counts. The Senate acquitted him on a party-line vote.

The White House released its own summary of the July 25, 2019 call — a rough transcript that it believed would exonerate Trump. It did not. The transcript confirmed that Trump said "I would like you to do us a favor, though" after Zelensky mentioned buying weapons. It confirmed that Trump asked Zelensky to investigate Burisma Holdings, where Hunter Biden had served on the board. It confirmed that Trump asked Zelensky to investigate a debunked conspiracy theory — that it was actually Ukraine, not Russia, that interfered in the 2016 election. The transcript was supposed to be the defense. Instead, it corroborated the whistleblower's complaint almost exactly.

"There was a quid pro quo. It was: get these investigations started and we'll arrange the meeting with the president. It was also: if you don't do that, you won't get the military assistance."

— Ambassador Gordon Sondland, Trump's own appointee and donor, testifying under oath before the House Intelligence Committee, November 20, 2019

What Ukraine Was Being Asked to Do — and What Was Being Withheld.

Ukraine at the time was engaged in an active armed conflict with Russian-backed separatists in its eastern Donbas region. The $391 million in security assistance — including Javelin anti-tank missiles specifically — was congressionally appropriated military aid, approved by bipartisan majorities, that Ukraine needed to defend itself. Trump placed a hold on the aid in early July 2019, more than two weeks before the call. Zelensky's team didn't learn the aid was being withheld until September 1, when a US aide informed them at a meeting in Warsaw. The GAO — a nonpartisan federal watchdog — later concluded that the withholding violated the Impoundment Control Act, which prohibits the executive branch from refusing to spend congressionally appropriated funds. The administration broke the law to create leverage over an ally fighting a Russian invasion.

Who Corroborated It — Under Oath.

Ambassador Gordon Sondland — a Trump donor and political appointee — initially testified he had no knowledge of a quid pro quo. After other witnesses contradicted him, he amended his testimony and said there was one. He described the arrangement explicitly: White House meeting and security aid conditioned on Ukraine publicly announcing the investigations. Lieutenant Colonel Alexander Vindman, the top Ukraine expert on the National Security Council who listened to the call in real time, testified that words were deliberately left out of the White House's published transcript — including Trump asking about recordings of Biden and Zelensky's mention of Burisma. Ambassador Bill Taylor, the top US diplomat in Ukraine, testified he was told directly that both the White House meeting and the military aid were conditioned on the announcements. State Department official Kurt Volker initially denied any quid pro quo — then amended his testimony after other witnesses spoke.

The Timeline.

July 18, 2019

Trump administration withholds $250 million in Defense Department security assistance to Ukraine.

July 25, 2019 — The Call

Trump tells Zelensky: "I would like you to do us a favor, though." Asks about the DNC server, CrowdStrike, Biden, and Burisma. Zelensky, who needs the weapons and the relationship, responds positively and tells Trump his next prosecutor general "will look into the situation."

August 12, 2019

A CIA official files a formal whistleblower complaint with the Inspector General. The administration initially refuses to share it with Congress.

September 11, 2019

After weeks of media reporting and congressional pressure, the administration releases the military aid to Ukraine — after the scheme was exposed, not because of any policy change.

September 24, 2019

Speaker Nancy Pelosi announces a formal impeachment inquiry. The White House releases its "rough transcript" of the call, believing it exonerates Trump. It doesn't.

December 18, 2019

The House of Representatives votes to impeach Trump on two articles: abuse of power (230-197) and obstruction of Congress (229-198). He becomes the third president in US history to be impeached.

February 5, 2020

The Senate votes to acquit on both counts. The vote on abuse of power is 52-48 to acquit — with Republican Sen. Mitt Romney being the only senator to vote to convict a president of his own party in US history.

January 16, 2020 — GAO Ruling

The Government Accountability Office concludes that the White House violated the Impoundment Control Act by withholding the congressionally approved Ukraine security assistance. The administration ignored the ruling.

Why It Matters in 2026.

Ukraine is now fighting a full-scale Russian invasion — the same Russia that Trump sided with in Helsinki, the same Russia that is currently providing intelligence to Iran about US military positions in the ongoing war. In February 2025, Trump held a meeting with Zelensky in the Oval Office that Zelensky himself later said reminded him of the pressure he felt in 2019. Trump has repeatedly suggested Ukraine should cede territory to Russia. The pattern of treating Ukraine as a bargaining chip — something to be leveraged for personal gain or strategic retreat rather than defended as an ally — runs unbroken from the July 25, 2019 phone call to the present day.
