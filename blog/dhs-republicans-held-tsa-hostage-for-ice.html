March 25, 2026 DHS shutdown ICE fact-checked

Republicans Held TSA Hostage for ICE. Congress Only Noticed When Their Airport Fast Lane Disappeared.

For 40 days, Republicans refused to fund TSA, FEMA, the Coast Guard, and CISA — because Democrats won't write a blank check for rogue immigration agents. ICE never stopped getting paid. Congress finally paid attention when Delta suspended the service that lets members of Congress skip security lines.

← all posts

Here is a simple fact that cuts through six weeks of noise: ICE agents have been getting paid this entire time. The $75 billion Republicans jammed into their reconciliation bill last year gave ICE a separate funding stream that the shutdown doesn't touch. The people without paychecks are TSA agents. FEMA workers. Coast Guard crews. Cybersecurity staff. The people you actually need when planes need screening and hurricanes hit.

Republicans have been holding all of those people hostage — not because they're fiscally responsible, not because of a principled stand — but because Democrats won't fund an immigration enforcement operation that has been shooting people in the streets.

Why the Shutdown Started in the First Place.

This didn't come from nowhere. The DHS shutdown started after ICE and Border Patrol agents killed two people during Operation Metro Surge — the Trump administration's immigration enforcement crackdown in Minneapolis. Alex Pretti was shot and killed by Border Patrol agents. Renee Good was also killed by federal agents. It was the 9th ICE shooting since September 2025.

Democrats said they would not fund the agencies responsible for those killings without reforms — no masks, judicial warrants required to enter homes, basic accountability. Republicans said no. Shutdown began.

Senator Patty Murray — on the Senate Floor

"Democrats are not going to write a blank check for rogue agencies that are trampling on the rights of Americans. Nor are we going to accept the premise that the only way to fund TSA or disaster relief is by giving money to ICE. That's absurd." Source: Senate Appropriations Committee

What Republicans Blocked — In 24 Hours.

Bills Republicans Blocked in a Single Day
  • A bill to fund all of DHS except ICE and CBP while negotiations continue — blocked by Sen. Katie Britt (R-AL), who called it "defunding police"
  • A bill to pay TSA workers while broader negotiations continue — blocked
  • A bill to fund FEMA — blocked
  • A bill to fund CISA (the cybersecurity agency) — blocked
  • A bill to fund the Coast Guard — blocked

Senator Britt's response to the proposal to fund everything except ICE: "We're not going back to the era of defund police." TSA agents are not police. FEMA is not police. The Coast Guard is not police. None of those agencies have anything to do with immigration enforcement. But sure.

Congress Woke Up When Their Fast Lane Got Taken Away.

Congress got "really interested" in fixing the DHS shutdown only after Delta suspended the special service that allows members of the House and Senate to skip security lines at airports. — The American Prospect

For five weeks, regular Americans stood in three-hour lines. Over 450 TSA agents quit. Highest wait times in TSA history. Congress: unmoved. Then Delta told senators they'd have to wait in line like everybody else. Suddenly: momentum. Suddenly: optimism. Suddenly: a framework emerging.

The emerging deal would fund all of DHS except ICE's deportation operations — essentially what Democrats had been offering for five weeks. Republicans plan to fund ICE later through reconciliation, which only needs 51 votes and would let them also slip in the SAVE America Act — the voter suppression bill — without any Democratic support.

The Part That Should Infuriate You.

ICE already has $75 billion from the "One Big Beautiful" reconciliation bill. ICE agents are being paid. The agencies being held hostage — TSA, FEMA, Coast Guard, CISA — are the ones that protect ordinary Americans from terrorism, natural disasters, and cyberattacks. None of them have anything to do with the immigration fight.

Republicans are holding disaster relief and airport security hostage to protect an agency that killed nine people in six months and faces zero accountability. And when a deal finally started to take shape, the catalyst wasn't the three-hour TSA lines. It was Congress losing its airport fast lane.
